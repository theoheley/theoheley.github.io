Modifications

Short Shifter

I’ve fitted a short shifter to sharpen up the gear changes — it’s a really cost-effective upgrade, and honestly makes a big difference whether you’re building a track car or just want a more engaging daily. The throws are much shorter, giving a more precise, mechanical feel.

MeisterR ZetaCRDs

I’m running MeisterR ZetaCRD coilovers — they’re ideal for fast road use and the occasional track day. They really help cut down body roll and keep the car feeling composed, but without ruining the ride comfort. Plus, with adjustable damping and ride height, I’ve got full control over how the suspension’s set up whether I’m on the street or at the track.

15" BBS Wheels

I’ve upgraded to a set of rare OEM 15" BBS wheels, which were an optional extra on some later MX-5s. They’re lightweight, forged, and look period-correct while shaving unsprung weight compared to most aftermarket options. I’m running them with Michelin Pilot Sport 3 tyres — a solid choice for fast road use, with loads of grip and predictable handling in both wet and dry. The move from the stock 14s to 15s gives sharper turn-in and a bit more feedback without ruining the ride quality.

Cobalt Cat-back Exhaust

I’m running a Cobalt stainless steel cat-back exhaust — it’s a direct-fit system that adds a bit more character without being obnoxious. The tone is deeper and sportier than stock, but still civilised enough for daily use. Build quality is solid, with proper welds and a polished finish that holds up well over time. It also frees up a bit of flow, which pairs nicely with intake and header upgrades down the line.

Big(ish) Brake Kit

I’ve fitted the big brake setup from a Mk2.5 Sport — it’s a solid OEM+ upgrade that bolts straight on. The front discs are 270mm (up from the NA’s 255mm), and the larger single-piston calipers offer improved heat capacity and stopping power, especially useful for track days or hard road driving. It’s a noticeable improvement in braking performance and pedal feel, especially when paired with braided lines, decent pads, and fresh fluid.

Invicta Roll Bar

I’ve fitted an Invicta Motorsports roll bar — it’s a serious safety upgrade and a must if you're planning any track time or just want extra peace of mind on the road. It’s designed to fit under the factory soft top with no cutting, and it ties into the chassis at multiple points for added rigidity. Compared to style bars, this is the real deal — proper DOM tubing, solid welds, and compliant with track day regulations. It also stiffens up the rear a bit, which helps with chassis feel.
